About Me

My name is Saleem Asfour and I am currently a Freshman at the University at Buffalo (UB). My current major is in Computer Science and I am planning to minor in business management. My father always had a love for computers and that is why he went to college for Computer Science. Unfortunatly, he had some financial problems while attending SUNY Old Westbury and was not able to finish and get his degree. He shared his passion of computers with me when I was at a young age and I loved every single part of it. My family got its first computer in 2004 and I remember sitting for hours everyday in front of it either playing games or watching my dad work. I would love it when he would sit down next to me and teach me whatever he knew. When I graduate in 2020, I am not doing it just for myself, I am also doing it for my father who was never able to accomplish his goal.

Once I graduate, my goal would be to apply to graduate schools and hopefully I can get my Masters in Computer Science. That plan is not set in stone, but thats what I would like to accomplish before getting a full-time job. After I pay out all my loans, my dream would be to move to Dubai, where I have a bunch of family and work there for the rest of my life.

Background Information

I was born in the Middle Eastern country of Jordan. It was not till I was 3 years old that my family decided to move to New York. At first we lived in the city, but a year later we moved to Yonkers. While I lived in the United States, I still did not know how to speak English. Arabic was my first language till I was taught English when I was 5 years old. I attended two schools when I was 5. I attended an elementary school and an Arabic teaching school. I moved to Syracuse, NY in 2007 and I lived there till 2016. I participated in the sport of wrestling when I was in 7th grade and continued all the way till 12th grade. Unfortunantly, I ended up snapping my arm in half at a Varsity Wrestling Tournament. I do not like taking pictures but there will be some in the gallery section along with pictures and x-rays of my arm after the wrestling mishap in January of 2016.
